RUN MACHINE OF INDIA

About The Legend

  • Virat, in full Virat Kohli, was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, to Prem Kohli and Saroj Kohli.
  • He has an older brother, Vikash, and an older sister, Bhavna.
  • Kohli's father was a criminal lawyer and his mother was a housewife.
  • He was raised in a Punjabi Hindu family in Delhi.
  • Kohli's interest in cricket began when he was just three years old, and his father would often play cricket with him in their backyard.
  • He joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy in 1998, where he was coached by Rajkumar Sharma.
  • Kohli's talent was soon recognized, and he was selected to play for the Delhi Under-15 team in 2002.
  • In 2006, he made his first-class debut for Delhi against Tamil Nadu.
  • Kohli's breakthrough performance came in 2008, when he scored 90 against Karnataka in the Ranji Trophy.
  • He made his international debut in 2008, playing for India in an ODI against Sri Lanka.
  • In 2011, he was part of the Indian team that won the ICC Cricket World Cup.
  • In 2013, he was appointed as the vice-captain of the Indian ODI team and later became the captain in 2017.
  • Under his captaincy, India won several series and tournaments, including the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy.
  • Kohli has been awarded several accolades, including the ICC Cricketer of the Year, ICC Test Player of the Year, and ICC ODI Player of the Year.
  • He has also been named as one of the Wisden Cricketers of the Decade in 2019.
  • Kohli is known for his aggressive batting style and has been compared to cricket legends like Sachin Tendulkar and Sir Donald Bradman.
  • He is also a successful entrepreneur and philanthropist, and has been involved in several charitable initiatives.
  • In 2017, he was ranked as the world's 10th highest-paid athlete by Forbes.
  • In 2020, he was ranked as the world's 26th highest-paid athlete by Forbes.
